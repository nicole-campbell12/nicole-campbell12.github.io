Shortcut Cocktail - The Bellini

It was the last weekend of our 2-week holiday break and I knew Monday was going to be rough. To close things out on a high note, we planned on going to a nice al fresco brunch at a place that serves our favorite Bellini cocktail.

Plans changed when the Big Joe furniture sets I ordered for our backyard arrived much sooner than expected…like 3 weeks sooner….and I was too excited to wait until we got back from brunch to set it all up (I love it so much - post coming soon). Also, the delivery guy stacked all 11 boxes in front of our garage door, so we were blocked in anyway!

Long story short, we stayed home and enjoyed a homemade brunch with Bellinis on the new comfy couches in our backyard.

A traditional Bellini is made with fresh or frozen peach puree. We didn’t have any peaches, but we did have a bottle of Simply Peach juice - hello shortcut!

Some notes:

  • This recipe is for a batch (you’re welcome)…I use a 1 liter juice carafe like this one.
  • I used regular vodka but you can make it with a peach-flavored vodka instead!
  • I prefer a Brut (aka dry / not sweet) champagne - the peach juice adds a good amount of sweetness. Some of my go-to choices (for cocktails) are Tott’s Brut Champagne (pictured), Alma Brut, and Veuve de Vernay.
  • If you don’t have or like vodka, no worries! It’s still pretty great with just the bubbles and peach juice.

Okay - Bellini time!

Shortcut batch Bellini

Approximately four (8oz) servings

  • 1 (750 mL) bottle of white sparkling wine or champagne
  • 3 oz vodka
  • Simply Peach juice

Preparation - Pour all of the champagne into the carafe, slowly…going too fast will result in overflowing bubbles! Add the vodka and slowly fill to the top with peach juice. Give it a gentle stir (I use the handle of a wooden spoon) and enjoy!
